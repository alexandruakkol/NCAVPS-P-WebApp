About NCAVPS/P

NCAVPS, simply put, measures the net worth of a share. NCAVPS only uses the current financial worth of the company. By dividing NCAVPS to the price, and thus obtaining NCAVPS/P, we create an environment in which fundamentals (NCAVPS) get separated from sentiment (P).

How to interpret NCAVPS/P

Higher is better. NCAVPS/P is a measure of net share value, relative to the share price. Thus, an 80% NCAVPS/P indicates that, for each $1 of share price, the owner gets $80c of net value, while the rest of $20c comes from non-fundamentals (sentiment, growth) and non-current assets.
